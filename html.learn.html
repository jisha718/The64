Anatomy of the Army

Infinite Value

King

chess_king

The most important piece. Moves one square in any direction. Protect him at all costs.

Cannot be captured, only checkmated.
Crucial in the endgame for opposition.
Value: 9 Points

Queen

chess_queen

The ultimate warrior. Combines the powers of the Rook and Bishop. High mobility.

The most versatile piece on the board.
Best kept active in the center.
Value: 3 Points

Knight

chess_knight

Moves in an 'L' shape and can jump over other pieces. Unpredictable and dangerous.

Excels in closed positions with many pawns.
Deadly at delivering forks.
Value: 5 Points

Rook

chess_rook

Moves in straight lines horizontally or vertically. Powerful in the endgame.

Strongest on open files without pawns.
Cooperate best in pairs on the 7th rank.
Value: 3 Points

Bishop

chess_bishop

Moves diagonally any number of squares. Limited to squares of its own color.

Powerful long-range snipers in open games.
The 'Bishop Pair' is a significant advantage.
Value: 1 Point

Pawn

chess_pawn

The soul of chess. Moves forward but captures diagonally. Small but significant.

Can move two squares on its first move.
Promote into any piece upon reaching the end.

Special Rules & Logic

Castling

The only move involving two pieces at once to secure the King.

En Passant

The 'in passing' capture rule that every beginner must learn.

Checkmate

The ultimate goal. Trapping the King with no escape possible.

Stalemate

A draw where no legal move exists but the King is not in check.
